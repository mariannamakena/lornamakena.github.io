Bunyala wards flood eposure, Busia County

Summary

The Bunyala Wards flood exposure assessment in Busia County was conducted by ICHA where the data team and voluntees travelled to Busia for a week with the aim of identifying schools, hospitals, villages, and other areas prone to flooding. Volunteers performed fieldwork using the KOBO toolkit questionnaire to locate these areas.

Objectives

  • To assess flood exposure in Bunyala wards, focusing on critical infrastructure and vulnerable communities.
  • To utilize volunteers for field data collection using the KOBO toolkit.
  • To map flood risks to inform disaster preparedness and response strategies.

Methods

Community Engagement: Engaged local communities through workshops and training on participatory mapping techniques.

Field Data Collection: Collected data using GPS devices and smartphones on infrastructure, hazards, and resources.

Findings

Geospatial Distribution: Revealed varying levels of flood risk across Bunyala wards, with some areas having higher flood exposure.

Critical Infrastructure: Identified schools, health facilities, and key village locations within high-risk flood zones.

Population Impact: High population density areas coincided with high flood risk zones, indicating significant community impact.

Flood Extent Mapping: Flood extent values ranged from low (1) to high (5), providing a visual representation of flood risks.
