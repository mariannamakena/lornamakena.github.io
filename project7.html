Disease Outbreak Snapshot

Summary

Disease outbreak snapshots are one-page visual summaries that highlight the scale, impact, and geographic spread of disease outbreaks within humanitarian crises. They are designed to support rapid decision-making, resource mobilization, and coordination among humanitarian actors.

These snapshots typically include:

  • Maps showing the geographic distribution of disease cases (e.g., cholera, measles, mpox, Ebola).
  • Graphs and tables illustrating:
    • Number of cases and deaths
    • Trends over time
    • Comparisons across countries or regions
  • Icons and infographics to represent:
    • Affected populations (e.g., children, displaced persons)
    • Health system capacity and gaps
    • Funding needs vs. funding received
  • Narrative summaries that contextualize the outbreaks within broader humanitarian challenges (e.g., conflict, displacement, food insecurity).
