Humanitarian Response Dashboard

Summary

It is an interactive data visualization platform that provides a real-time overview of how humanitarian needs are being addressed in a specific crisis or country. It is typically aligned with the Humanitarian Response Plan (HRP) and updated regularly to reflect progress, gaps, and funding status.

The dashboard typically includes:

  • 5Ws Data (Who, What, Where, When, Whom)
    • Who: Organizations involved
    • What: Activities or services provided
    • Where: Geographic locations of interventions
    • When: Timeframes of activities
    • Whom: Targeted and reached beneficiaries
  • Sector/Cluster Breakdown
    • Progress by sector (e.g., Health, WASH, Shelter, Protection)
    • Number of people targeted vs. reached
    • Key indicators per cluster
  • Funding Overview
    • Total funding requirements vs. received (linked to FTS)
    • Sector-specific funding gaps
    • Donor contributions
  • Geographic Visualization
    • Interactive maps showing activity coverage
    • Hotspots of unmet needs or overlapping interventions
  • Infographics and Key Figures
    • People in need, displaced, affected
    • Demographic breakdowns (e.g., children, women, elderly)
