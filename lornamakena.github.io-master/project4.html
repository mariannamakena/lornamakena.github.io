Garissa Farms Drone Images

Summary

The Garissa Farms Drone Images project aimed to process drone images for areas where people had settled in Garissa as a result of evacuating their homes due to El Ni√±o floods. By leveraging drone technology and image processing techniques, the project aimed to provide valuable insights for planning and disaster response efforts.

Objectives

  • Process drone images to assess the extent of settlement in Garissa farms.
  • Identify areas of high population density to prioritize relief efforts.
  • Map out infrastructure and access routes for effective disaster response planning.

Methods

Data Collection:

Utilized drone technology to capture high-resolution images of Garissa farms and surrounding areas.

Image Processing:

Applied image processing techniques to analyze drone images and identify settlements, infrastructure, and land use patterns.

Mapping:

Generated maps and spatial datasets to visualize the distribution of settlements and infrastructure for disaster response planning.

Findings

Settlement Patterns: Identified clusters of settlements in Garissa farms, indicating areas of high population density.

Infrastructure Mapping: Mapped out existing infrastructure such as roads and buildings to facilitate relief efforts and resource allocation.

Disaster Preparedness: Provided valuable data for disaster preparedness and response planning to mitigate the impact of future floods and other natural disasters.
