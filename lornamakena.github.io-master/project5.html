Participatory Mapping by ICHA

Summary

The Participatory Mapping organized by ICHA focused on using OpenStreetMap (OSM) and JOSM for uploading data and adding OSM tags in collaboration with local communities. The project aimed to empower communities to contribute to mapping efforts, improving the accuracy and inclusivity of geographic data for disaster preparedness and response. A mapathon is a coordinated mapping event whereby participants can join physically or remotely to add map data on a particular area through a web based mapping platform. The idea is that small contributions from many mappers who have localized knowledge of the area will ultimately lead to improved maps and data that organizations can use for decision making before and after a disaster. The mapathon will be open to new and experienced mappers from different backgrounds. Objectives

  • Engage local communities in mapping their own areas using participatory methods.
  • Train community members on using JOSM and OSM for data uploading and tagging.
  • Enhance the accuracy and completeness of geographic data related to infrastructure, hazards, and resources.

Methods

Community Engagement:

Organized workshops and training sessions to educate community members on participatory mapping techniques.

Field Data Collection:

Utilized GPS devices and smartphones to collect field data on infrastructure, hazards, and resources.

Data Uploading and Tagging:

Trained participants on using JOSM and OSM platforms to upload collected data and add relevant OSM tags.

Findings

Community Empowerment: Empowered local communities to actively contribute to mapping efforts and take ownership of their geographic data.
