Households affected by floods mapping and KRCS Reponse during El Nino

Summary

This involved a comprehensive mapping of households affected by floods during the El Niño event in Bunyala wards. By leveraging geospatial analysis, the team identified high-risk areas and assessed the impact on local households. This data was crucial for the Kenya Red Cross Society (KRCS) to deploy resources efficiently and provide timely assistance to the most affected communities. The findings underscore the importance of accurate and timely data in disaster response and management.

Objectives

  • Identify Affected Households: To map the households affected by floods during the El Niño event in Kenya.
  • Assess Impact: Evaluate the extent of the impact on these households to inform emergency response and recovery efforts.
  • Coordinate KRCS Response: Facilitate the Kenya Red Cross Society's (KRCS) response by providing accurate data on affected areas and households.

Methods

Data Analysis and Mapping: Analyzed the collected data using GIS software to create detailed maps indicating flood exposure and impacted households.

Collaboration with KRCS: Worked closely with KRCS to ensure the data was utilized effectively for disaster response and resource allocation.

Findings

Geospatial Analysis: The mapping revealed significant flood exposure in several households across Kenya, highlighting areas with high vulnerability.

Impact Assessment: A substantial number of households were found to be at high risk, necessitating immediate intervention and long-term mitigation measures.

Population Impact: High population density areas coincided with high flood risk zones, indicating significant community impact.

KRCS Response: The data supported KRCS in coordinating their relief efforts effectively, ensuring resources were allocated to the most affected areas promptly.
