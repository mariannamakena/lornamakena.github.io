Buildings at Risk of Flooding within Nairobi in a 30m Buffer

Summary

Buildings at Risk of Flooding within Nairobi in a 30m Buffer is an Instant App that identified at-risk buildings and provided a valuable tool for enhancing flood preparedness and response.

Objectives

To identify buildings within Nairobi that are at risk of flooding, specifically those within a 30-meter buffer zone of Nairobi rivers.

Develop an application that allows users to quickly and easily access information about buildings at risk of flooding.

Enhance disaster preparedness by equipping city planners, emergency responders, and residents with valuable data to improve flood preparedness and response.

Leverage geospatial data and tools to conduct the analysis and present findings in a user-friendly format.

Methods

Data Collection:

Geospatial Data: Gather data on Nairobi's building footprints, water bodies, and flood-prone areas from sources such as satellite imagery, and open-source GIS datasets.

Flood Risk Zones: Define flood risk zones using historical flood data, hydrological models, and topographical information.

Data Processing:

Buffer Analysis: Use GIS software to create a 30-meter buffer zone around identified water bodies and flood-prone areas.

Overlay Analysis: Overlay building footprints with the buffer zones to identify buildings within the at-risk areas.

Findings

Risk Distribution: The analysis revealed a significant number of buildings within the 30-meter buffer zone, indicating high potential flood risk in specific neighborhoods.

High-Risk Areas: Certain areas, especially those along major rivers and low-lying regions, were identified as high-risk zones with dense building footprints.
