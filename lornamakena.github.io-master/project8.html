Kenya Red Cross: Emergency operations projects.

Summary

At KRCS:EOC I was responsible for Collecting, managing, and organizing geographic data relevant to emergency response operations. This included maps, satellite imagery, and GPS coordinates, as well as attribute data related to disaster-prone areas, infrastructure, population demographics, and resources. Created maps and conducted spatial analysis to identify high-risk areas, vulnerable populations, and critical infrastructure. Used GIS software to visualize spatial patterns, trends, and relationships that can inform decision-making during disaster response, recovery and management. Helped identify optimal locations for emergency shelters, medical facilities, and distribution centers based on population distribution and accessibility for disaster preparedness e.g. flooding events. Performed spatial analysis to derive meaningful insights from geospatial data in themes of conflict, post-election violence, flood prone areas and road accidents.

Findings

Geospatial Analysis: The mapping revealed significant flood exposure in several households across Kenya, highlighting areas with high vulnerability.

Impact Assessment: A substantial number of households were found to be at high risk, necessitating immediate intervention and long-term mitigation measures.

Population Impact: High population density areas coincided with high flood risk zones, indicating significant community impact.

KRCS Response: The data supported KRCS in coordinating their relief efforts effectively, ensuring resources were allocated to the most affected areas promptly.
