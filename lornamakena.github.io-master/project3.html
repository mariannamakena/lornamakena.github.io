Mabatini and Mlango Kubwa wards, Mathare Disaster Risk Reduction

Summary

The Mabatini and Mlango Kubwa wards, Mathare Disaster Risk Reduction project aims to mitigate the risk of disasters eg fires, drugs, insecurity and gender based violence, within the Mathare area of Nairobi. By implementing various strategies and leveraging geospatial data, the project seeks to enhance preparedness and response mechanisms.

Mabatini Ward

  • Fire Hazard: Due to overcrowded living conditions and informal electrical connections, Mabatini faces a high risk of fire hazards.
  • Gender-Based Violence (GBV): Poverty, unemployment, and overcrowding contribute to increased vulnerability to GBV, particularly affecting women and children.
  • Mental Illness: Challenges related to mental health are prevalent in Mabatini due to various stressors such as poverty and violence.
  • Insecurity: Crime, gang activities, and drug abuse are significant concerns in Mabatini, impacting the safety and well-being of residents.

Mlango Kubwa Ward

  • Fire Hazard: Similar to Mabatini, Mlango Kubwa faces fire hazards due to overcrowded living conditions and infrastructure challenges.
  • Gender-Based Violence (GBV): Socio-economic factors contribute to heightened risks of GBV in Mlango Kubwa, particularly impacting women and children.
  • Mental Illness: Residents of Mlango Kubwa may face mental health challenges stemming from poverty, trauma, and limited access to mental health services.
  • Insecurity: Insecurity is prevalent in Mlango Kubwa, with crime rates, gang activities, and substance abuse posing threats to community safety.

Objectives

To identify vulnerable communities and infrastructure within Mathare prone to risks.

To develop and implement strategies for disaster risk reduction, including early warning systems and evacuation plans.

To engage local stakeholders and communities in disaster preparedness and response activities.

Methods

Data Collection:

Gather geospatial data on topography and OSM building footprints, and infrastructure such as hospitals and schools within Mathare.

Vulnerability Assessment:

Analyze factors contributing to vulnerability, such as population density, infrastructure, and environmental conditions.

Community Engagement:

Work closely with local communities to understand their needs and capacities for disaster preparedness.

Findings

Vulnerability Hotspots: Identified specific areas within Mathare with higher vulnerability, including informal settlements and low-lying areas.

Community Resilience: Despite challenges, observed resilience and coping mechanisms among local communities, which can be further supported and enhanced through targeted interventions.
