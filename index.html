About

Hi there! I'm Lorna Makena, a passionate Geospatial Information Science (GIS) analyst who loves working where geography meets technology. My journey began at Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology, where I built a strong foundation in GIS, spatial analysis, Python, and R programming, Remote Sensing processes, Cartography and Data Science. I specialize in turning complex spatial data into clear, actionable insights, whether it's mapping disaster-prone areas or creating data-driven plans for urban development. Every project is like a new puzzle for me, and I enjoy finding creative solutions through data science.

As an Information Management Assistant with the United Nations Office for the Coordination of Humanitarian Affairs (UNOCHA) under the UN Volunteer (UNV) programme, I support data-driven humanitarian coordination through geospatial analysis, mapping, and reporting. Beyond this professional role, I am deeply committed to making a positive impact through volunteering. My experiences with the International Center for Humanitarian Affairs (ICHA) and my internship at the Kenya Red Cross Society's Emergency Operations Center have allowed me to apply my GIS skills to real-world challenges. From managing disasters to building community resilience, I use my knowledge to help those in need. Volunteering is more than just a commitment for me; it's a passion that continually inspires both my professional and personal growth.
